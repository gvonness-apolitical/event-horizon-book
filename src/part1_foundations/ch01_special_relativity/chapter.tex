\chapter{Special Relativity: A Working Primer}
\label{ch:special-relativity}

This chapter reviews the formalism of special relativity that underpins
everything that follows. We establish the index notation, metric signature, and
observer-decomposition techniques used throughout the book, with particular
emphasis on the null vectors and tetrad constructions that reappear in the ray
tracer's camera model.

\section{The Minkowski Metric, Four-Vectors, and Index Gymnastics}
\label{sec:sr-minkowski}

We introduce the Minkowski metric $\eta_{\mu\nu}$ in the $({-}{+}{+}{+})$
convention, define four-vectors, and establish the index-raising and -lowering
operations that generalise to curved spacetime in later chapters.

\section{Lorentz Transformations, Boosts, and Observer Tetrads}
\label{sec:sr-lorentz}
\coderef{Spacetime}{Minkowski}

We derive the Lorentz group from the invariance of the interval, specialise to
boosts, and construct the orthonormal tetrad of an arbitrary inertial observer
--- the same construction used to define the ray tracer's camera frame.

\section{Light Cones, Causal Structure, and Null Vectors}
\label{sec:sr-causal}

We define the causal structure of Minkowski spacetime, classify vectors as
timelike, spacelike, or null, and show how null vectors parametrise the
directions on an observer's sky --- the starting point for ray tracing.

\paragraph{Key References.}
The index-notation conventions follow \cite{MTW1973}; the tetrad formalism
draws on \cite{Wald1984}. The camera-tetrad construction is described in
\cite{James2015}.
