\chapter{General Relativity}
\label{ch:general-relativity}

With the geometric toolkit in hand, this chapter presents Einstein's field
equations and their physical content. We discuss the stress-energy tensor, the
weak-field limit that recovers Newtonian gravity, and survey the exact
solutions that the codebase implements.

\section{The Einstein Field Equations}
\label{sec:gr-efe}

We motivate and state the Einstein equations $G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi T_{\mu\nu}$,
discuss their structure as second-order PDEs for the metric, and outline the
role of the Bianchi identity in enforcing energy-momentum conservation.

\section{Stress-Energy and Matter Coupling}
\label{sec:gr-stress-energy}

We define the stress-energy tensor for a perfect fluid and for the
electromagnetic field, showing how matter sources enter the field equations.

\section{The Weak-Field Limit}
\label{sec:gr-weak-field}

We linearise the Einstein equations around flat spacetime, recover the
Newtonian potential, and derive the gravitational redshift---an effect visible
in the ray tracer's output.

\section{Exact Solutions: An Overview}
\label{sec:gr-exact-solutions}

We survey the exact solutions implemented in the codebase---Schwarzschild,
Kerr, and Morris-Thorne---motivating the detailed treatments in the following
