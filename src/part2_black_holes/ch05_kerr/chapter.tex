\chapter{The Kerr Solution}
\label{ch:kerr}

Astrophysical black holes carry angular momentum. This chapter presents the
Kerr metric, its horizon and ergosphere structure, and the coordinate systems
used in the codebase. We also derive the ISCO formulae that set the inner edge
of accretion disc models and briefly discuss the Morris-Thorne wormhole as an
alternative spacetime.

\section{Boyer-Lindquist Coordinates}
\label{sec:kerr-bl}
\coderef{Spacetime}{Kerr}

We write the Kerr metric in Boyer-Lindquist form, identify the coordinate
singularities, and define the functions $\Delta$, $\Sigma$, and $A$ that
recur throughout the chapter.

\section{Kerr-Schild Form}
\label{sec:kerr-ks}

We transform to Kerr-Schild coordinates, which are horizon-penetrating and
used as the codebase's default representation for ray tracing through the
ergosphere.

\section{The Boyer-Lindquist Radius Quartic}
\label{sec:kerr-bl-quartic}

We derive the quartic equation that converts between Kerr-Schild and
Boyer-Lindquist radial coordinates and discuss its numerical solution.

\section{Horizons, Ergosphere, and Frame Dragging}
\label{sec:kerr-horizons}

We locate the inner and outer horizons, define the ergosphere, and quantify
frame dragging through the angular velocity of zero-angular-momentum
observers.

\section{ISCO Formulae}
\label{sec:kerr-isco}

We derive the spin-dependent ISCO radius for prograde and retrograde orbits,
connecting to the accretion disc inner edge used by the renderer.

\section{The Morris-Thorne Wormhole\starmark{}}
\label{sec:kerr-wormhole}
\coderef{Spacetime}{MorrisThorne}

We briefly present the Morris-Thorne traversable wormhole metric as an example
of a non-black-hole spacetime implemented in the codebase, discussing its
throat geometry and geodesic structure.

\paragraph{Key References.}
The Kerr metric treatment follows \cite{Visser2007} and \cite{Wald1984}; ISCO
formulae are from \cite{Bardeen1972}; the wormhole section draws on
\cite{MorrisThorne1988}.
