\chapter{ODE Integration: Runge-Kutta Methods}
\label{ch:ode-integration}

Every dynamical equation in this book --- the geodesic equation for ray
tracing, the BSSN evolution system for numerical relativity, the gauge
conditions that tie coordinates to the geometry --- has the form of an
initial value problem: a state, a rule for computing its rate of change,
and the need to advance forward reliably.  The previous chapter supplied
the spatial-derivative operators that feed into these right-hand sides;
this chapter builds the time-stepping machinery that turns a right-hand
side into a trajectory.

For the ray tracer, the challenge is sharpened by the physics.  A single
image requires tracing millions of photons through a Kerr spacetime, and
each photon's journey spans wildly different dynamical regimes: nearly
straight propagation far from the black hole, where the curvature is
negligible and large steps suffice, and tight spirals near the photon
sphere, where trajectories are exponentially sensitive to perturbations
and the integrator must take short, careful steps to stay on track.  No
single fixed step size spans this range efficiently.  The Dormand--Prince
embedded Runge--Kutta pair resolves the tension by computing two solutions
of different order from the same intermediate stages, producing an error
estimate at negligible extra cost.  An adaptive step-size controller uses
this estimate to grow $h$ when the trajectory is smooth and shrink it
when accuracy demands --- concentrating computational effort exactly
where the dynamics require it.

The chapter builds the integrator in layers: the general Runge--Kutta
framework and Butcher-tableau notation (\cref{sec:rk-butcher}), the
specific Dormand--Prince 5(4) pair (\cref{sec:rk-dormand-prince}), the
FSAL optimisation that saves one function evaluation per accepted step
(\cref{sec:rk-fsal}), the adaptive controller that adjusts $h$ on the
fly (\cref{sec:rk-adaptive}), and the mapping of every abstract
component onto the Hamiltonian geodesic system
(\cref{sec:rk-geodesic-application}).
