\chapter{Spectral Methods}
\label{ch:spectral-methods}

Spectral methods achieve exponential convergence for smooth problems by
representing functions as global polynomial expansions. This chapter develops
the Chebyshev and Legendre frameworks used in the codebase's discontinuous
Galerkin solver, covering interpolation, differentiation matrices, quadrature,
and the modal-nodal duality.

\section{Polynomial Interpolation}
\label{sec:spec-interpolation}

We review Lagrange interpolation on general node sets, introduce the Runge
phenomenon, and motivate the use of clustered node distributions.

\section{Chebyshev Polynomials and Gauss-Lobatto Nodes}
\label{sec:spec-chebyshev}
\coderef{spectral}{rs}

We define the Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind, derive the
Gauss-Lobatto node distribution, and show that it near-optimally suppresses
interpolation error.

\section{Differentiation Matrices}
\label{sec:spec-diffmat}

We construct the polynomial differentiation matrix that maps function values
at collocation nodes to derivative values, and discuss its conditioning.

\section{Legendre Polynomials and Gaussian Quadrature}
\label{sec:spec-legendre}

We introduce Legendre polynomials, derive Gauss and Gauss-Lobatto quadrature
rules, and establish the exactness conditions for polynomial integrands.

\section{Spectral Convergence}
\label{sec:spec-convergence}

We state and illustrate the exponential convergence theorem for spectral
methods applied to smooth functions, contrasting with the algebraic
convergence of finite differences.

\section{Legendre-Gauss-Lobatto Quadrature}
\label{sec:spec-lgl}
\coderef{dg}{rs}

We detail the LGL quadrature rule and its role in the mass-lumping
approximation used by the discontinuous Galerkin scheme.

\section{Vandermonde Matrices and Modal-Nodal Representations}
\label{sec:spec-vandermonde}

We define the Vandermonde matrix that transforms between modal (expansion
coefficient) and nodal (point value) representations, and discuss its use in
filtering and limiting.

\paragraph{Key References.}
The spectral-methods framework follows \cite{Hesthaven2007} and
\cite{Kopriva2009}; Chebyshev theory draws on \cite{Trefethen2000};
the DG application follows \cite{HesthavenWarburton2008}.
