\chapter{3+1 ADM Decomposition}
\label{ch:adm-decomposition}

The Arnowitt-Deser-Misner (ADM) formalism recasts Einstein's field equations as
an initial-value problem by decomposing four-dimensional spacetime into a family
of three-dimensional spatial hypersurfaces evolving in time. This chapter
develops the 3+1 split that underpins every numerical relativity evolution scheme
in the book.

\section{Foliation of Spacetime}
\label{sec:adm-foliation}

We define a foliation as a one-parameter family of spacelike hypersurfaces
$\Sigma_t$ and introduce the unit normal vector $n^\mu$ that threads them
together.

\section{Lapse, Shift, and the Spatial Metric}
\label{sec:adm-lapse-shift}
\coderef{ADMSpacetime}{typeclass}

We decompose the line element into lapse function $\alpha$, shift vector
$\beta^i$, and spatial three-metric $\gamma_{ij}$, explaining the gauge
freedom each encodes.

\section{Extrinsic Curvature}
\label{sec:adm-extrinsic-curvature}

We define the extrinsic curvature $K_{ij}$ as the Lie derivative of the spatial
metric along the normal, and relate it to the time derivative of $\gamma_{ij}$.

\section{The ADM Evolution and Constraint Equations}
\label{sec:adm-equations}

We derive the Hamiltonian constraint, momentum constraint, and the evolution
equations for $\gamma_{ij}$ and $K_{ij}$, and discuss why the ADM system as
written is only weakly hyperbolic.
