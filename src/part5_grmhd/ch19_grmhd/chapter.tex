\chapter{General-Relativistic MHD}
\label{ch:grmhd}

With the flat-space MHD machinery in hand, we now couple the fluid to a
dynamical or fixed curved-spacetime background. This chapter develops the
Valencia formulation of GRMHD, the metric coupling strategy, and the
practical atmosphere treatment required for vacuum regions.

\section{Valencia Formulation}
\label{sec:grmhd-valencia}
\coderef{mhd\_grid}{rs}

We write the GRMHD equations in the Valencia form, identifying the conserved
variables, fluxes, and geometric source terms that arise from the
non-vanishing Christoffel symbols.

\section{Metric Coupling}
\label{sec:grmhd-metric}
\coderef{mhd\_metric}{rs}

We explain how the spatial metric, lapse, and shift enter the MHD fluxes
and source terms, and describe the data flow between the spacetime evolution
and the MHD solver.

\section{Atmosphere Treatment}
\label{sec:grmhd-atmosphere}

We describe the artificial atmosphere floor applied in near-vacuum regions
to prevent negative densities and conservative-to-primitive failures, and
discuss the criteria for atmosphere activation.

\section{Method of Lines with RK4\starmark{}}
\label{sec:grmhd-mol}

We present the method-of-lines semi-discretisation that decouples spatial and
temporal integration, and detail the fourth-order Runge-Kutta time stepper
used for the combined spacetime--matter system.

\paragraph{Key References.}
The Valencia formulation follows \cite{Banyuls1997,Font2008}; the atmosphere
treatment draws on \cite{Etienne2015}; the method of lines follows
\cite{Hesthaven2007}.
