\chapter{Accretion Disk Physics}
\label{ch:accretion}

With the GRMHD solver operational, we turn to the astrophysical initial
conditions and emissivity models that produce the images of accreting black
holes. This chapter covers analytic disk models and the radiation-transport
ingredients needed for visualisation.

\section{Novikov-Thorne Thin Disk}
\label{sec:acc-novikov-thorne}
\coderef{AccretionDisk}{hs}

We derive the Novikov-Thorne model for a geometrically thin, optically thick
disk: the radial profile of angular velocity, specific energy, flux, and
effective temperature as functions of the Kerr spin parameter.

\section{Fishbone-Moncrief Torus}
\label{sec:acc-torus}
\coderef{torus}{rs}

We construct the Fishbone-Moncrief equilibrium torus as initial data for
GRMHD simulations, specifying the constant angular momentum distribution and
inner edge location.

\section{Emissivity Models}
\label{sec:acc-emissivity}
\coderef{emissivity}{rs}

We implement bremsstrahlung and synchrotron emissivity formulae, combine them
via Kirchhoff's law for thermal emission, and discuss the integration of the
radiative transfer equation along geodesics for image rendering.

\paragraph{Key References.}
The Novikov-Thorne model follows \cite{Novikov1973,Page1974}; the
Fishbone-Moncrief torus follows \cite{Fishbone1976}; synchrotron emissivity
draws on \cite{Leung2011}; the radiative transfer integration follows
\cite{Dexter2009}.
